What Is Sandbox Réseau?

A calm, intentional meetup where builders learn in public and find collaborators.

From the Event Brief

Sandbox Réseau brings together builders, thinkers, and makers shaping the next wave of innovation in Africa. It celebrates connection, collaboration, and creation.

Developers, designers, founders, and product lovers share work, showcase prototypes, and learn from practical lessons in the open.

Format at a Glance

Three simple blocks that keep attention on the work.

  • Builder Demos — 5–7 minutes on live projects or prototypes. Decisions, trade-offs, next steps.
  • Mini-Talks — compact insights from product, design, and engineering.
  • Open Network — generous time to meet collaborators and swap ideas.

Why It Exists

Signal over noise; progress over polish.

“No hype. Just signal.”

Réseau creates a trusted room to learn in public with feedback, momentum, and a network that actually ships.

Who’s It For?

Engineers, designers, founders, data/ML folks, indie makers — and the product-curious.

Beginner-friendly by design. Curiosity and a builder’s mindset are enough.

Details & Logistics

When: Dates announced on Luma. Check schedule.

Where: Accra. Venue shared with confirmed guests to keep the room intentional.

Capacity: Kept tight for focused conversations; early RSVP recommended.

Demos: Indicate interest on RSVP; we curate 3–5 per edition.

Code of Conduct: Be kind, be specific, credit work, respect time.

FAQ

  • Beginner-friendly? Yes.
  • Finished product required? No — prototypes welcome.
  • Pitches? Keep it useful and grounded; salesy pitches discouraged.
  • Recordings? We prioritize candour; we’ll publish notes where possible.
  • Cadence? Recurring — watch the Luma page.
